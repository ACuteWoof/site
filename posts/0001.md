# On childishness, equitability, and the final cause

In the modern day, it appears, that while we proclaim with pride our
indifference about things that matter in reality, we bicker about those things
that are quite insignificant. I have a notion that the described childish
attitude may be explained by the forsaking of the idea of the final cause.

I wish to first address possible objections that the voices in my head pose
playing the devil's advocate. The first of the objections being the proposition
that one should care and act in some way about any _cause_ (this word plays an
important role later in demonstrating a particular cognitive dissonance of the
modern man) that he is made aware of, even if his act be harmful or irrelevant
to said cause. Let us first note that the proposition implicitly states, with
the word "should", that said act is obligatory, and good.
