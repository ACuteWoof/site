Useful links

These are some links to pages that I find very useful. Some are pretty well known, some are pages I’ve never heard of before finding them after hours of searching for something very specific. I have not sorted the links in any way or grouped them yet. I will get to it eventually (hopefully).

You can also find more useful content on my Kiwix instance: https://kiwix.lewoof.xyz.

  • https://consumerrights.wiki/ - Louis Rossmann's consumer rights wiki
  • https://wiki.futo.org/ - FUTO Wiki (guide to a self managed life)
  • https://en.wikibooks.org/ - public domain books
  • https://www.gutenberg.org/ - public domain books
  • https://osdev.org/ - information on developing operating systems
  • https://standardebooks.org/ - very beautifully formatted public domain E-books (available to download in multiple formats and to read online)
  • https://lukesmith.xyz/articles/learning-european-languages-michel-thomas/ - article on learning languages in an easier and more natural way
  • https://lib.undercaffeinated.xyz/ - someone’s calibre library I found
  • https://www.ccel.org/ - christian classic writings, including early church fathers, post and ante Nicene
  • https://www.integral-calculator.com/ - integral calculator
  • https://farside.link/ - automatically redirects any url to its appropriate private alternative frontend
  • https://dumb.ducks.party/ - alternative and simpler frontend for Genius the lyrics website
  • https://awesome-selfhosted.net/ - list of self-hosted alternatives to popular services
  • https://github.com/mendel5/alternative-front-ends?tab=readme-ov-file - list of alternative frontends
  • https://github.com/digitalblossom/alternative-frontends - another list of alternative frontends
  • https://sr.ht/~jamesponddotco/awesome-privacy-front-ends/ - another list of alternative frontends
  • https://libredirect.github.io/ - another list of alternative frontends and extension that autoredirects to those alternatives
  • https://github.com/bkerler/mtkclient - exploits for MediaTek chips
  • https://docs.invidious.io/instances/ - list of invidious instances
  • https://nitter.net - alternative frontend for Twitter, for anyone who for whatever reason wants to use Twitter
  • https://kiwix.org/ - download and host Wikipedia and other sites that collect and store knowledge
  • https://fmhy.net/ - free media heck yeah
  • https://artvee.com/c/landscape/ - beautiful landscape art
  • https://tineye.com/ - reverse image search
  • https://www.privacyguides.org/en/ - guides on privacy, list of alternatives, etc.
  • https://oss.zone/ - very cool pubnix, I use their instance of 4get and I love it
  • https://gobyexample.com/ - Learn Go (programming language) with examples
  • https://git-scm.com/book/en/v2 - git book
  • https://notrelated.xyz/ - Luke Smith's podcast, it's entertaining; remember never to take any "podcaster" or "youtuber" or "influencer" (the titles are so stupid they make me want to puke) as infallible
  • https://httpstatusdogs.com/ - dog pics for different HTTP status codes
  • https://http.cat/ - cat pics for different HTTP status codes
  • https://www.feynmanlectures.caltech.edu/ - The Feynman Lectures on Physics
